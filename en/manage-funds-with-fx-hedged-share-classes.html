                            Manage funds with FX-hedged share classes

                            FX hedging is used by the funds that have share classes denominated in other currency than the fund itself. It allows the fund to neutralize the effect of the exchange rate between the fund base currency and the share class currency. For this, the fund uses FX Forward contracts that balances the share class currency exposure.

                            In FA, FX hedging is set up and handled in the following way:

                            • Setting up the fund and share classes. You need to set up the fund and share classes in FA Fund management app, create accounts and FX Forward contracts.

                            • NAV calculation and hedge accrual. When NAV is calculated, the system calculates the change in the FX Forward market value and creates a share class-related “accrued FX Hedge” (HDG) transaction to accrue the amount to the Fund portfolio account. FA Fund management app shows accrued FA Hedging in the NAV calculation.

                            • FX contract settlement. When the FX Forward is settled, the system calculates profit and loss from FX Forward contracts and creates a cash in transaction to the fund bank account.

                            Setting up accounts

                            Add the following accounts to the fund settings in the Fund wizard (FA Fund management app):

                            • Share class accounts in the share class currency. Select Shared to all parent portfolio's subportfolios option.

                            • Share class-specific accrual accounts for Accrued FX Hedge (HDG) transactions to the Fund portfolio. These accrual accounts should be in the fund's base currency.

                            Setting up fund and share classes

                            A fund with hedged share classes is represented by a two-level portfolio structure that you can view in FA Back. The portfolio structure includes the Fund portfoio and FX hedge portfolios per share class on the lower level and a parent portfolio that links them together on the higher level. The FX hedge portfolios contain FX Forward and FX Swap contracts. The parent portfolio is empty and serves only to for grouping the fund and FX hedge portfolios.

                            To set up a two-level portfolio structure for a fund with FX-hedged share classes:

                            1. Create a fund in FA Fund management app. In the Fund wizard, enter the FX hedge settlement settings in the Fund fees tab:

                              • Fee formula. The formula to settle FX hedging: FA hedge settlement offset formula.

                              • Fee transaction type. Transaction type to use for FX hedging settlement: Settle FX Hedge (HDGS).

                              Choose the Formula sets its own account option.

                            2. Create share classes in the FA Fund management app. In the Share class wizard, choose the following settings in the FX Hedging tab:

                              • FX hedge transaction type. Transaction type set up in the system to accrue FX hedge. Choose Accrued FX Hedge (HDG).

                              • Portfolio type. FX hedge portfolio.

                              Click Create hedge portfolio and choose the formula for the FX hedge accrual (HDG). I'll describe this in more detail when the fature is available in fadev.

                            As a result, FA creates the following structure in FA Back:

                            • Fund structure portfolio: ABC Fund parent

                              • Fund portfolio: ABC Fund

                              • FX hedge portfolio: ABC Fund share class A hedged

                              • FX hedge portfolio: ABC Fund share class B hedged

                            FX hedging for share classes

                            Enter the FX contracts for hedging of each share class in the relevant FX hedge portfolio for the share class. For details about FX contracts, see FX contracts in FA User guide. In the contracts, add the shared accounts in the share class currency that you created (see Setting up accounts).FX contracts
